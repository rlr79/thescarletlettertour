by Nathaniel Hawthorne

published 1850 C.E.
20 years after the Mayflower landed, during the early Colonial Era in the US, the Puritans collectively inhabited the area presently known as Boston, Massachusetts.
This is the setting for The Scarlet Letter - a story which was heavily influenced by Nathaniel's own upbringing in New England, and his family's ties to religous Puritans, including his great-great-great grandfather, William Hathorne, who was a Puritan that immigrated from England to Massachusets, and his great-great grandfather, John Hathorne, who presided as a judge over the religious persecution of local residents in the Salem Witch Trials.

Boston, Massachusetts
